Proud Moments of Indian Sports

India has had several proud moments in sports, such as Neeraj Chopra's historic gold in javelin at the Tokyo 2020 Olympics and the Indian men's cricket team's World Cup victories. PV Sindhu, Mary Kom, and Hima Das have also made their mark globally, bringing home medals and accolades. In team sports, the Indian men's hockey team’s bronze in the 2020 Olympics and the Indian women’s volleyball team's silver in the BRICS Games further highlight the country’s sporting achievements.

“India Women Volleyball team won Silver Medal & Men secured Bronze Medal in the BRICS Games at Johannesburg, South Africa.” The Indian Women’s Volleyball team achieved a remarkable milestone by winning the Silver Medal, while the Men’s team secured the Bronze Medal at the BRICS Games held in Johannesburg, South Africa. This achievement highlights the growing strength of Indian volleyball on the international stage and showcases the dedication and talent of the athletes. Their impressive performances at the prestigious event not only earned them podium finishes but also brought pride to the nation.
“2018 Commonwealth Games, 26 Gold, 20 Silver and 20 Bronze” At the 2018 Commonwealth Games held in Gold Coast, Australia, India delivered an outstanding performance, securing a total of 66 medals: 26 Gold, 20 Silver, and 20 Bronze. This achievement marked India's best-ever medal haul at the Commonwealth Games, showcasing the country’s strength in sports like wrestling, weightlifting, badminton, and boxing. The impressive results highlighted the progress of Indian athletes on the global stage and reinforced the country's growing sporting reputation.
“22nd Asian Athletics Championships 2017, 12 Gold and 5 Silver and 12 Bronze” At the 22nd Asian Athletics Championships held in Bhubaneswar, India, in 2017, the Indian team delivered an exceptional performance, securing a total of 29 medals: 12 Gold, 5 Silver, and 12 Bronze. This remarkable achievement highlighted India's growing prominence in athletics, with athletes excelling in events like javelin throw, high jump, and the 400m hurdles. The championship was a significant milestone, showcasing the country’s potential in track and field events on the Asian level.
“2016 Rio Olympics, 1 Silver and 1 Bronze Medals” At the 2016 Rio Olympics, India achieved notable success, winning a total of 2 medals: 1 Silver and 1 Bronze. The Silver medal was won by P.V. Sindhu in badminton, making her the first Indian woman to win an Olympic silver in an individual event. The Bronze medal was secured by wrestler Sakshi Malik, who became the first Indian female wrestler to win an Olympic medal. These historic performances were significant milestones in Indian sports, further inspiring future generations of athletes.
“2014 Asian Games, 11 Gold and 10 Silver and 36 Bronze” At the 2014 Asian Games held in Incheon, South Korea, India achieved a remarkable medal haul of 11 Gold, 10 Silver, and 36 Bronze, finishing in 8th place overall. This performance marked a significant improvement in India’s standing at the Asian Games, with athletes excelling in sports like wrestling, boxing, and badminton. The diverse range of medals reflected the growing talent and potential of Indian athletes across multiple disciplines, further boosting the country's sports reputation on the continental level.
“2014 Commonwealth Games, 15 Gold and 30 Silver and 19 Bronze” At the 2014 Commonwealth Games in Glasgow, Scotland, India delivered a strong performance, winning a total of 64 medals: 15 Gold, 30 Silver, and 19 Bronze. This impressive achievement highlighted the country's dominance in sports like wrestling, weightlifting, badminton, and boxing. The 2014 Games marked a significant milestone, with India finishing 5th on the medal table and showcasing the growing strength of its athletes on the international stage.
“2012 London Olympics, 2 Silver and 4 Bronze Medals” At the 2012 London Olympics, India won a total of 6 medals: 2 Silver and 4 Bronze. The Silver medals were earned by Vijay Kumar in shooting and Sushil Kumar in wrestling, while the Bronze medals were secured by Gagan Narang in shooting, Mary Kom in boxing, Yogeshwar Dutt in wrestling, and the men's hockey team. This marked India's best Olympic performance at the time, reflecting significant progress in sports like shooting, wrestling, and boxing.
